================================================================================
                    RASP (Randomly Accurate Soaring Predictions)
                              PARAMETER REFERENCE GUIDE
================================================================================

This document describes the meteorological parameters available in RASP forecasts,
primarily used for soaring flight planning (gliding, paragliding, hang gliding).

================================================================================
                           THERMAL PARAMETERS
================================================================================

wstar (Thermal Updraft Velocity)
    Unit: ft/min
    The average dry thermal updraft strength near the top of the usable boundary
    layer. Subtract 200 ft/min to get a more realistic in-flight climb rate.
    Values < 200 ft/min = weak/no thermals, 400-600 ft/min = moderate, > 800 ft/min = strong.

hbl (BL Top / Thermal Height)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height of the top of the mixing layer (boundary layer). This represents the
    expected maximum height of dry thermals. NOT the cloudbase height.

dbl (BL Depth)
    Unit: ft
    Depth/thickness of the boundary layer. Represents the vertical extent
    of the convective mixing layer from the surface to the BL top.

hglider (Thermalling Height)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Max thermalling height, which is the minimum of the BL top and the
    overdevelopment cloudbase. This is the practical ceiling for glider pilots.

dglider (Thermalling Depth)
    Unit: ft
    The expected depth/thickness of usable thermals for soaring. Calculated as
    hglider minus the height where thermals become too weak to use.

hwcrit (Critical Height)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height at which the thermal updraft strength falls below a critical threshold
    (typically 220 ft/min). Useful for determining minimum working altitude.

dwcrit (Critical Depth)
    Unit: ft
    Depth of thermals above the critical updraft threshold. The altitude band
    where thermals are strong enough to be usable for climbing.

bltopvariab (BL Top Variability)
    Unit: ft
    Spatial variability of the boundary layer top height. High values indicate
    inconsistent thermal heights across the region.

wblmaxmin (BL Max/Min W)
    Unit: ft/min
    Maximum grid-area-loss updraft strength at BL top. A negative value indicates
    overall sinking motion. Positive values suggest stronger thermals possible.

zwblmaxmin (Height of BL Max/Min W)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height at which the wblmaxmin value occurs. Useful for understanding the
    vertical structure of thermals.

================================================================================
                         THERMAL QUALITY PARAMETERS
================================================================================

bsratio (Buoyancy/Shear Ratio)
    Unit: dimensionless
    Ratio of thermal strength to wind shear. Indicates thermal quality:
    < 5 = broken/sheared thermals, 5-7 = moderate, > 7 = good organized thermals.
    Low values mean thermals will be tilted and harder to work.

wstar_bsratio (Combined Thermal Index)
    Combined display of wstar and bsratio for quick assessment of both thermal
    strength and quality in one parameter.

sfcshf (Surface Heat Flux)
    Unit: W/m²
    Surface sensible heat flux - the rate at which heat is transferred from the
    surface to the atmosphere. Higher values indicate stronger thermal generation.
    Negative values indicate heat transfer to the ground (cooling).

sfcsunpct (Surface Sun Percentage)
    Unit: %
    Percentage of full sunshine reaching the surface. Reduced by clouds.
    100% = clear sky, lower values indicate cloud shadowing.

================================================================================
                         CLOUD PARAMETERS
================================================================================

zsfclcl (Surface LCL Height)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Lifting Condensation Level based on surface conditions. The height where
    cumulus clouds would form if air is lifted from the surface.

zsfclclmask (Surface LCL Masked)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Same as zsfclcl but masked (not shown) where cumulus are not expected to form.

zsfclcldif (Cu Potential)
    Unit: ft
    Difference between the BL top and the surface LCL. Positive values indicate
    good potential for cumulus cloud formation (Cu overdevelopment possible).

zblcl (BL Cloud Height)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height of cloud base from BL-mean values. More representative of actual
    cloudbase than surface-based LCL during well-mixed conditions.

zblclmask (BL Cloud Height Masked)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Same as zblcl but masked where cumulus clouds are not expected.

zblcldif (BL Cu Potential)
    Unit: ft
    BL-based cumulus potential. Positive values indicate cumulus expected.

blcloudpct (BL Cloud Cover)
    Unit: %
    Percentage of the sky expected to be covered by cumulus clouds forming
    at the top of the thermals. High values may indicate overdevelopment.

blicw (BL Integrated Cloud Water)
    Unit: kg/m²
    Integrated cloud water content within the boundary layer. Indicates
    the total amount of condensed water in BL clouds.

blcwbase (BL Cloud Water Base)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height of the base of clouds containing significant cloud water.
    Can indicate where actual visible cloudbase will be.

cfracl / wrf=CFRACL (Low Cloud Fraction)
    Unit: %
    Fraction of sky covered by low-level clouds (below ~2km).

cfracm / wrf=CFRACM (Mid Cloud Fraction)
    Unit: %
    Fraction of sky covered by mid-level clouds (~2-6km).

cfrach / wrf=CFRACH (High Cloud Fraction)
    Unit: %
    Fraction of sky covered by high-level clouds (above ~6km).
    High cirrus can reduce surface heating and weaken thermals.

================================================================================
                           WIND PARAMETERS
================================================================================

sfcwind0 (Surface Wind)
    Unit: knots
    Wind speed and direction at the surface (10m AGL). Important for
    takeoff/landing assessment and ridge soaring potential.

blwind (BL Average Wind)
    Unit: knots
    Average wind speed and direction through the boundary layer.
    Represents the overall wind affecting thermal drift.

bltopwind (BL Top Wind)
    Unit: knots
    Wind at the top of the boundary layer. Important for assessing
    thermal drift at cloudbase and high-altitude conditions.

blwindshear (BL Wind Shear)
    Unit: knots per 1000ft
    Vertical wind shear through the boundary layer. High shear tilts
    thermals and makes them harder to work. Affects thermal quality.

================================================================================
                         SURFACE PARAMETERS
================================================================================

sfctemp (Surface Temperature)
    Unit: °C
    Air temperature at 2m above ground level. Important for thermal
    triggering and pilot comfort.

sfcdewpt (Surface Dew Point)
    Unit: °C
    Dew point temperature at 2m AGL. Indicates moisture content.
    Closer to sfctemp = higher humidity = lower cloudbase potential.

rain1 (1-hour Precipitation)
    Unit: inches
    Accumulated precipitation over the previous hour. Indicates
    shower/storm activity that will suppress thermal activity.

cape (Convective Available Potential Energy)
    Unit: J/kg
    Energy available for convection. High CAPE (>500 J/kg) indicates
    potential for strong thunderstorm development. Be cautious!
    0-500 = weak, 500-1500 = moderate, >1500 = strong instability.

================================================================================
                      PRESSURE LEVEL PARAMETERS
================================================================================

press950 / press955 (950/955 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 500m/450m ASL. Shows wind, temperature
    at low levels above ground.

press850 / press899 / press846 (850 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 1500m ASL. Standard level for synoptic
    analysis of lower troposphere.

press795 (795 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 2000m ASL.

press700 / press701 (700 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 3000m ASL. Important for mountain flying
    and assessing mid-level conditions.

press616 / press540 (600-500 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 4000-5500m ASL. Upper-level analysis.

press500 (500 hPa Level)
    Conditions at approximately 5500m ASL. Standard level for synoptic
    analysis of mid-troposphere.

================================================================================
                         CONVERGENCE PARAMETERS
================================================================================

wblmaxmin (BL Max Upward/Downward Motion)
    Unit: ft/min
    Maximum upward or downward vertical motion within the boundary layer,
    indicating areas of wind convergence (positive/lifting) or divergence
    (negative/sinking). Convergence zones can enhance thermal lift and mark
    areas where thermals are more likely to form or strengthen.
    Positive values = convergence/lift, negative = divergence/sink.

zwblmaxmin (Height of BL Max W)
    Unit: ft AGL
    Height at which the maximum boundary layer vertical motion (wblmaxmin)
    occurs. Helps understand where in the boundary layer the convergence
    or divergence is strongest.

================================================================================
                      CROSS-COUNTRY PERFORMANCE PARAMETERS
================================================================================

pfd_tot (Potential Flight Distance Total)
    Unit: km
    Estimated achievable cross-country flight distance based on thermal
    conditions, averaged over the forecast period. Higher = better soaring day.

xcspeed (Cross-Country Speed)
    Unit: km/h
    Expected achievable cross-country speed based on thermal strength and
    spacing. Accounts for time spent climbing and gliding between thermals.
    Higher values indicate faster XC potential and better soaring conditions.
    Is based on a Discus glider polar.

stars (Star Rating)
    Unit: 0-5 stars
    Overall soaring quality rating combining thermal strength, height, and 
    conditions. Quick visual indicator of expected soaring conditions:
    0-1 = poor/unsoarable, 2 = marginal, 3 = moderate, 4 = good, 5 = excellent.

================================================================================
                         SOUNDING PARAMETERS
================================================================================

sounding1, sounding2, etc.
    Atmospheric soundings (skew-T diagrams) at specified locations.
    Shows vertical profile of temperature, dew point, and wind.
    Essential for detailed analysis of atmospheric stability.

    Custom or experimental parameter. Check documentation for specific

================================================================================
                         PARAMETER INTERPRETATION TIPS
================================================================================

GOOD SOARING CONDITIONS:
• wstar > 500 ft/min
• hbl > 5000 ft AGL
• bsratio > 5
• blcloudpct 10-40% (scattered Cu)
• Low wind shear
• cape < 500 J/kg (avoid thunderstorms)

MARGINAL CONDITIONS:
• wstar 200-400 ft/min
• bsratio 3-5
• High wind shear
• Very low or very high cloudbase

POOR/DANGEROUS CONDITIONS:
• wstar < 200 ft/min
• bsratio < 3
• blcloudpct > 60% (overdevelopment)
• cape > 1500 J/kg (thunderstorm risk)
• rain1 > 0 in forecast area
